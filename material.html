Phishing Awareness:

Phishing awareness is the understanding and knowledge of phishing assaults that people and organizations have, as well as the methods and practices they employ to prevent being a victim of such attacks. Phishing is a type of cyberattack in which perpetrators attempt to trick victims into divulging sensitive data, such as usernames, passwords, credit card numbers, and other personal or financial information, by assuming the identity of a reliable entity in emails, messages, or websites. In order to take advantage of human weaknesses, phishing attempts frequently use psychological manipulation and social engineering techniques, hence it is crucial for people and organizations to be knowledgeable and attentive.

Phishing:

Phishing is a type of cyberattack in which attackers try to trick victims into disclosing private or sensitive information, including usernames, passwords, credit card numbers, or other sensitive information. This is sometimes accomplished by pretending to be a reliable organization or body, such as a bank, social media site, or even a coworker or acquaintance. Phishing attacks can happen via a variety of channels of communication, including emails, texts, social media posts, and even phone calls.

Types of Phishing:

1.Spear Phishing:

While spear phishing is a far more targeted attack in which the hacker is aware of the precise person or company they are pursuing, classic phishing is a "spray and pray" strategy in which mass emails are sent to as many recipients as possible. In order to personalize the attack and raise the chance that the target will fall for their trap, they conduct research about the target.

2.Session Phishing:

In session hijacking, a phisher takes advantage of the web session control mechanism to steal the user's information. The phisher can use a sniffer to intercept pertinent data during a straightforward session hacking technique known as session sniffing in order to gain unauthorized access to the Web server.

3.Email Spam

Millions of individuals receive the identical email with a request to enter personal information using the most popular phishing tactic. The phishers will exploit this details for their unlawful operations. The user must input their credentials in order to update account information, amend data, or verify accounts in the majority of communications since they include an urgent note. They might occasionally be required to complete a form in order to access a new service via a link in the email.

4.Content Injection:

Using the content injection approach, a phisher alters a portion of the content on a page of a trustworthy website. This is done to trick the visitor into leaving the legitimate website and going to a page where they are then prompted to provide personal information.

5.Web Based Delivery

One of the most sophisticated phishing strategies is web-based delivery. The hacker, also referred to as "man-in-the-middle," is situated between the legitimate website and the phishing system. During a transaction between the user and the real website, the phisher tracks information. Without the user's knowledge, the phishers continue to collect information as the user sends it to them.

6.Phishing Through Search Engines

Some phishing schemes employ search engines to steer users to websites that might promise inexpensive goods or services. The phishing site gathers the user's credit card information when they attempt to purchase the item using that method. There are a lot of bogus bank websites that advertise low-interest credit cards or loans to visitors but are actually phishing sites.
